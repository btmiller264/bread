Focaccia Bread Recipe

from The Food Network

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 cups warm water
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1 TB sugar
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for kneading
  • 1 TB kosher salt, plus coarse sea salt for sprinkling
  • 1 cup extra virgin olive oil (divided)

Directions

  1. Mix together the warm water, sugar and yeast in a bowl. Put the bowl in a WARM (not hot or cool) place until the yeast is bubbling and you can smell it (at least 15 min).
  2. Mix together the flour, 1 TB of kosher salt, 1/2 cup olive oil and the yeast mixture. Works best if you have a kitchenaid with a dough hook, and turn it on low speed. Once it all comes together, up the speed to medium for 5 to 6 minutes until it is smooth and soft. Sprinkle with flour if it is sticky.
  3. Pour the dough out onto a clean, lightly floured surface and knead it by hand 1 or 2 times. Keep sprinkling with flour if sticky.
  4. Coat your mixer bowl lightly with olive oil and put the dough back in the bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and put it in a warm place til the dough doubles in size - will at least be an hour.
  5. Coat a jelly roll pan with 1/2 cup olive oil. (Yes, all 1/2 cup. Focaccia is oily.)
  6. Slap the dough in that pan and press it around so it fits the size of the pan. Then turn it over to make sure all the dough is coated with oil. Keep stretching the dough to fit the pan, and as you do so, spread your fingers out to make finger holes all the way through the dough. (This is what gives the bread it's characteristc look!)
  7. Put the pan in a warm place till it has doubled in size again (around 1 hour). While the dough is rising a second time, preheat the oven to 425F.
  8. Sprinkle some sea salt on the top of the dough and drizzle some olive oil on there. Bake it til the top is golden brown (usually 25 to 30 min). Let it cool before cutting or serving!
  9. Enjoy the sweet bready goodness.
